# Experience Report - Luca

## What parts of the exercise were confusing?

The biggest thing that confused me was how specific the document is supposed to be. The worked examples show like exact hex colors and pixel values for borders, but the exercise also says "no graphical mockups required". So am I supposed to make up colors and exact CSS values? That felt weird because I'm writing a text document not coding. I didnt know how much visual detail to invent.

Also the "meta-level" thing in the beginning about the document being an AI prompt was confusing. I understand the idea but I wasnt sure if that changes what I should write or if its just motivation.

The advanced requirements section - I wasnt sure if I had to do any of them or not. It says "pick the ones that interest you" but I didnt know if the teacher expects us to do at least one.

## What was easy to understand?

The scenario itself was clear. A cinema, seats, booking - I understood what the system should do. The venue facts table was really helpful, I could see exactly what the cinema looks like. The business rules were also clear and specific.

The structure with the 8 sections was nice because I knew what topics to cover. Without that I would not know where to start.

## Where did I get stuck?

I got stuck the most on Seat States. The worked example shows a really detailed description for ONE state and I was supposed to do that for ALL states. But I dont even know how many states there are. The exercise says "think beyond just available and booked" but how many more? I came up with like 5 but maybe there are more and I dont know.

I also got stuck on the error and edge cases section. I can think of some obvious ones but the exercise says things like "what if another user books a seat you've selected" and I described it but not in much detail. Its hard to describe exactly what happens on screen when I'm not looking at a real interface.

The accessibility section was hard too. I know what wheelchair seats are but designing the flow for how someone indicates they need accessibility - that could go many ways and I didnt know how detailed to be.

## What would you change about the exercise?

- Give more worked examples. Two is good but maybe show a COMPLETE short section, not just one state and one step. I want to see what a finished section looks like.
- Tell us exactly how many seat states we need to define. "Think beyond available and booked" is vague. Maybe give a minimum number.
- The self-check questions at the end are helpful but they made me feel like my document is never good enough. Maybe add a "minimum viable" checklist so we know when we've done enough for a passing grade.
- The estimated effort says 4-8 hours. I think for me its more like 6-10 hours because I spent a lot of time re-reading the exercise trying to understand what level of detail is expected.
- Maybe provide a more filled-in template with placeholder text showing the expected length for each section.

## How long do you think this would take in real life?

I think it would take me about 7-8 hours realistically. I spent a lot of time just understanding what was expected. The actual writing goes okay once I know what to write, but figuring out what counts as "detailed enough" is the hard part. I would probably write it, then read the self-check, realize its not enough, and then not know what to add.

## Did the worked examples help?

Yes, they were the most helpful part of the exercise. Without them I would have written much less detail. The first example (seat state) showed me that I need colors, what triggers the state, and what the user can do from there. The second example (booking flow step) showed me that each step needs to describe what the user sees and does.

But I still felt like my writing was less detailed than the examples. The examples set a high bar and I was not sure I could match it for every single section. Its like seeing a perfect answer and then having to write 8 more at that level - thats a lot.

## Did the guiding questions help?

Kind of. They pointed me in the right direction like "what does the user see first when the map loads" - that made me think about something I would have forgotten. But some of them felt like they were asking the same thing as the section description, just worded differently. The one about "could a developer implement it from your description alone" made me feel bad because the answer is probably no for some of my sections.

## Other observations

- I think this exercise is more suited for people who like writing. As someone who prefers coding, I would rather just build the thing and show it. Writing about how it SHOULD work without building it feels backwards to me.
- The seating plan image was helpful. Without it I would not understand the layout at all.
- I kept wanting to write code or pseudocode instead of descriptions. Like for the seat states, my brain wanted to write an enum and switch statement, not paragraphs.
- The quality bar ("could a developer build this without asking questions") is scary. Thats a very high bar. I think even professional designers dont always hit that.
- I noticed I sometimes just copied phrases from the exercise like "seats are held for 8 minutes" without really designing what that looks like for the user. Its hard to not just repeat the requirements back.
